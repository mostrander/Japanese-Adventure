Museums

Visiting a museum can be a great way to introduce oneself to a new culture and familiarize with the culture's historical background. Below are two well-known museums in Tokyo, Japan.

Ghibli Museum:

Fans of Hayao Miyazaki's work will adore the Ghibli Museum because it is dedicated to the no longer existing Studio Ghibli. It showcases many of the company's artwork from their films, as well as concept art and occasional special exhibitions. Note, however, that tickets can only be purchased in advance via reservation because they are not sold at the museum itself. More Information

Tokyo National Museum:

This is the oldest national museum in Japan and is the best place to see historical artwork and artifacts from Japan that cannot be seen anywhere else, including national treasures. Comprised of six different buildings, Tokyo National Museum frequently offers special exhibitions. Furthermore, they offer English translations for their exhibits! More Information
