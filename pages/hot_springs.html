Onsen (Hot spring)

One of the best ways to relax in Japan. These ponds of warm water are not only a great way to relax, but also provide several health benefits including reducing muscle pain. They can be gender segregated or gender mixed, so be careful which onsen you choose if you do not want to be naked in front of the opposite sex! Note: Most onsen ban anyone with tattoos from entry, so ask the staff before paying if you have a tattoo!

Niwa no Yu:

This hot spring is well known for its beautiful landscapes that surround their ornate pools of warm water, and it is an excellent escape for parents because children are not allowed.

Spa LaQua:

This spa is located within Tokyo Dome City and offers multiple types of hot spring baths, including open air baths that allow visitors to listen to the sounds outside as they bathe. They also offer saunas, massages, a women-only lounge, a nail salon, and the ability to try the latest cosmetic items from famous brands after purchasing a cheap brush set! It is open throughout the night, but does not admit anyone with tattoos.

Saya no Yudokoro:

A hot spring surrounded by beautiful garden landscaping and is highly recommended by previous visitors.
