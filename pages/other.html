Other Locations

There are many other places to visit in Tokyo, Japan. Below are two other popular locations individuals often visit when they stay in Tokyo. For other ideas please visit other sections of this website!

Imperial Palace:

Rebuilt after its destruction in World War ll, this is the official residence of the Japanese Imperial family. It features beautiful architecture, moats, and gardens just a short distance away from Tokyo train station. Tours are available in English year-round. The inner palace grounds are only accessible on January 2 and December 23, and this is the only time when tourist will be able to see the Imperial family as well. This is a great place to visit for anyone interested in the Imperial family and their residence, especially at the start or end of the year.

Rikugien Garden:

This large 18th century landscape garden is one of the most popular gardens in Tokyo, Japan, especially in autumn. Designed to depict various scenes from famous Japanese poetry, this garden offers breathtaking scenery and several teahouses along the way for visitors to rest at. It is also a lovely place to visit in the early spring when Cherry Blossom season starts (March to April).
